Europe is heavily dependent on Russia for its oil and gas. In 2021, two-fifths of the gas Europeans burned came from Russia.

But two countries have stepped up their efforts to cut down on importing Russian gas. Since 2020, Malta and Lithuania have significantly reduced reliance on Russian gas. Lithuania says it will stop importing gas from Russia as of April.

From Luxembourg to Austria, EU member states are planning to ramp up renewables, increasing their energy resources. The option also includes mitigating the higher energy costs to vulnerable consumers and small businesess.

To prepare for any cutoffs as the war between Russia and Ukraine intensifies and to reduce Russian reliance, countries are rounding up new supplies of liquefied natural gas — LNG — by ship.

They're also speeding up plans for gas import terminals and pipelines that don't depend on Russia and talking about allowing coal-fired power plants to keep spewing climate-changing emissions for longer if it means energy independence.
