Stanford researchers also said they observed that the threshold test to the available traffic stop data, indicated that police require less suspicion to search black and Hispanic drivers than white drivers. This double standard is evidence of discrimination.

Disentangling discrimination from policing is hard.

The Stanford Open Policing Project also found that stopped black and Hispanic drivers are searched more often than white drivers. However, researchers noted that if minorities also happen to carry contraband at higher rates, these higher search rates may stem from appropriate police work. Disentangling discrimination from effective policing is challenging and requires more subtle statistical analysis.
